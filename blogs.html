Municipal Solid Waste Statistics in Odisha: Key Insights

Odisha, with its population of over 45 million, faces significant waste management challenges. The state generates approximately 5,000 tons of municipal solid waste daily, with urban areas like Bhubaneswar and Cuttack contributing the majority. This waste comprises 50-60% organic matter, 15-20% plastics, and the rest paper, metals, and other materials.

Key statistics reveal that only 70% of generated waste is collected, with collection efficiency varying across ULBs. Ecolizer's interventions have improved collection rates to 95% in partnered areas, demonstrating the potential for comprehensive solutions. The economic value of recoverable materials exceeds ₹500 crores annually, yet much remains untapped due to inadequate segregation practices.

Seasonal variations, particularly during festivals, increase waste generation by 30-40%, straining existing infrastructure. Climate factors like monsoons further complicate collection and processing. Ecolizer's IoT-based systems provide real-time data to optimize operations during peak periods.

Future projections indicate waste generation will reach 8,000 tons daily by 2030, necessitating immediate investments in infrastructure and technology. Ecolizer's model of public-private partnerships offers a scalable solution to meet these growing demands while creating economic opportunities.
